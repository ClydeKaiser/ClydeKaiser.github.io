Place Your Bets

When betting on combat sports, you usually want to bet on something that doesn't require too much specifics, since fighting is one of the most unpredictable sports known to man. The safest bet you can make is a Moneyline bet. This bet simply is a prediction of who will win or lose. It doesn't get into the specifics of how the person will win, or how many strikes they throw. It's simply who wins the fight. A parlay bet is similar to this method but is adding a more volatile risk of losing money but creating a much higher payout at the same time. When making a parlay bet, you are betting on multiple winners of different fights. If you make five bets and four of your five bets win but one bet loses, you lose the entire bet. A four fight parlay usually payouts 10|1 odds which if you were to win a $100 parlay bet, you would get a return of $1,010. Sports betting odds typically will be in a decimal or positive/negative format. The decimal format is the most basic format in sports betting. A simple example would be if you were to win a $10 bet with 2.00 odds, your final payout would be $20. With positive or negative odds, a( +200) shows the potential profit on a $100 bet, whereas a negative number (-150) shows how much you need to bet to make $100.
